# LinkedIn newsletter CTA block, weekly insert

Drop this block into each weekly edition of The Transformation Brief on LinkedIn. Best placement is **immediately after the Lead Signal**, before On the Radar. That is the point in the issue where attention peaks and the reader is still committed. Putting it at the end loses the fold.

LinkedIn newsletter editor supports basic formatting: bold, italic, bullets, links, inline headers. The block below is designed to render cleanly in that editor.

---

## The block, paste this in

> **One-question pulse**
>
> This month we are measuring the Governance driver of the Blueprint. Your answer joins the Blueprint Index, which we publish in the next issue.
>
> *If a regulator asked tomorrow for a plain-language log of every AI agent decision your organisation has made this quarter, could you produce it?*
>
> **→ Answer in 15 seconds** (anonymous, no email): **[pulse.craighortonadvisory.com](https://pulse.craighortonadvisory.com)**
>
> The people who answer this week shape the number 180 CHROs read next week. Join the panel.

## Why the copy is shaped this way

1. **"One-question pulse" as the header.** sets expectation before the reader commits attention. 15 seconds, one question, no more.
2. **"We are measuring the Governance driver of the Blueprint."** frames the question as part of something structured, not a random survey. positions the Blueprint name in the reader's vocabulary every time.
3. **The question in italics.** signals it is the actual pulse, not prose to skim past.
4. **"Answer in 15 seconds (anonymous, no email)".** removes the two objections that kill click-through, time and privacy.
5. **"The people who answer this week shape the number 180 CHROs read next week."** gives the reader a reason that is not about helping Craig. they are shaping the reference number their peers will cite. social proof + agency.

No em-dashes. Sentence case except references (Blueprint, Blueprint Index, LinkedIn, CHRO). Matches brief brand voice.
