The Framework

The AI readiness blueprint

A diagnostic framework for assessing and accelerating AI transformation, grounded in six evidence-based drivers that determine whether organisations realise the full promise of AI, or stall at the last mile.

Driver 01

Strategic alignment

AI initiatives that are not directly connected to strategic intent fail to secure sustained leadership commitment. Alignment is not a kickoff slide, it is a governance operating model.

Driver 02

Leadership and culture

Transformation is sponsored from the top but stalls in the middle. The management layer, not the boardroom, is where AI initiatives live or die.

Driver 03

Talent and capability

Technical skills are the floor, not the ceiling. The capability gap most organisations face is in change leadership, not data science.

Driver 04

Data and technology

Data quality, integration, and governance are the infrastructure layer. Most organisations over-invest here while under-investing in the human architecture above it.

Driver 05

Change enablement

The last mile problem is a change management problem. Adoption, behaviour change, and trust-building determine whether deployed AI creates value or generates resistance.

Driver 06

Governance and risk

With the EU AI Act, GDPR, and emerging agentic frameworks, governance is no longer optional overhead. It is the condition under which AI earns organisational and regulatory trust.

This week's signal

Weekly intelligence for leaders navigating AI transformation, through the lens of management, governance, and behaviour, not technology.

Security Boulevard / Adversa AI 10/10

Half of enterprises cannot see what their AI agents are doing

The 1H 2026 State of AI and API Security Report confirms what governance advocates have feared: 48.9% of organisations are entirely blind to machine-to-machine traffic generated by their own AI agents, and 48.3% cannot distinguish legitimate agents from malicious bots. This is not a technology gap, it is a management failure. When nearly half of enterprises cannot answer the question "what did our agents do today?", the conversation is no longer about platform capability, it is about operational accountability. The BVF Governance and Risk dimension would score most of these deployments below threshold.

On the radar

Kennedys Law · 9/10

EU AI Act: four months to full enforcement, no grace period

August 2, 2026 is the date every transformation leader needs on their wall. Annex III high-risk AI systems become fully enforceable with penalties up to EUR 35 million or 7% of global turnover. Organisations still treating compliance as a parallel workstream rather than an integrated governance layer are running out of runway.

BCG Publications · 9/10

70% of AI value comes from organisational transformation, not technology

BCG's latest research quantifies what the best transformation leaders already know: roughly 10% of AI value comes from algorithms, 20% from the technology stack, and the remaining 70% from change management, workforce transformation, and organisational redesign.

Becker's Hospital Review · 9/10

Healthcare AI agents: kill switches, guardrails, and the debate over autonomy

Health system IT leaders are now actively debating how much autonomy to grant agentic AI. The smartest organisations are implementing full audit logs, real-time performance dashboards, strict permissions, and kill switches.

Microsoft · 8/10

Microsoft launches Agent 365 governance toolkit

Microsoft's release of Agent 365 signals that the hyperscalers now recognise agent governance as a product category, not an afterthought.

From: "How many agents can we deploy?" → To: "Can we account for the ones we already have?"

This week's signal is unmistakable. The enterprise world crossed a threshold sometime in the last quarter, the point where agent proliferation outpaced organisational visibility. Nearly half of enterprises cannot monitor their own agent traffic. Regulators are four months from enforcing penalties that assume you can produce audit logs on demand. Healthcare systems are debating kill switches because the alternative, agents acting without oversight, is now a clinical risk. And BCG has put a number on what happens when you invest in the technology without investing in the people and structures around it: you lose 70% of the value. The transformation leaders who will define the next phase are not the ones deploying fastest, they are the ones who can answer, with evidence, what their agents did today and why.

The reframing question

If a regulator, a board member, or a patient asked you for a plain-language summary of every autonomous decision your AI made this week, could you produce one by Friday?

The agentic era: I did it because no one told me not to

If you can't read the log, you can't govern the agent.

  1. How many agents are operating in our organisation today?
  2. Can we audit their actions in plain language?
  3. Do our associates have real, practical power to override the agents? Show me.
