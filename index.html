The Framework

The AI Readiness Blueprint

A diagnostic framework for assessing and accelerating AI transformation, grounded in six evidence-based drivers that determine whether organisations realise the full promise of AI, or stall at the last mile.

Driver 01

Strategic Alignment

AI initiatives that are not directly connected to strategic intent fail to secure sustained leadership commitment. Alignment is not a kickoff slide, it is a governance operating model.

Driver 02

Leadership And Culture

Transformation is sponsored from the top but stalls in the middle. The management layer, not the boardroom, is where AI initiatives live or die.

Driver 03

Talent And Capability

Technical skills are the floor, not the ceiling. The capability gap most organisations face is in change leadership, not data science.

Driver 04

Data And Technology

Data quality, integration, and governance are the infrastructure layer. Most organisations over-invest here while under-investing in the human architecture above it.

Driver 05

Change Enablement

The last mile problem is a change management problem. Adoption, behaviour change, and trust-building determine whether deployed AI creates value or generates resistance.

Driver 06

Governance And Risk

With the EU AI Act, GDPR, and emerging agentic frameworks, governance is no longer optional overhead. It is the condition under which AI earns organisational and regulatory trust.

This Week's Signal

Week Of 30 March 2026

The governance gap is the real bottleneck. This week's signals confirm that the constraint on AI value is not capability, it is the management layer designed for a pre-AI world.

Harvard Business Review Score 10/10

The "Last Mile" Problem Slowing AI Transformation

Enterprise AI initiatives are stalling not at the model level, but at the organisational boundary where technology meets management structure. Legacy processes, tribal knowledge, and governance gaps prevent adoption at the employee level, confirming that the bottleneck is the pace layer of organisational design, not the pace layer of technology.

On The Radar

PwC

The Consulting Model Just Changed

PwC deploys autonomous AI agents to deliver consulting work. Humans now govern rather than drive. The agentic consulting revolution is not coming, it has arrived.

EU Regulation

EU AI Act: 78% Unprepared, 4 Months To Go

Four months to compliance and nearly four in five enterprises have not completed their AI Act readiness assessment. Governance is now a deadline, not a strategy discussion.

BNY Mellon

20,000 AI Agents In Production

BNY Mellon is running 20,000 AI agents in live operations. This is not a pilot. The question for every enterprise is no longer "if" but "what governance model enables this at scale."

Gartner

CHROs Know The Gap. Few Are Closing It.

78% of CHROs agree roles and workflows must change. Only 34% of organisations produce measurable financial impact from AI. Awareness is not transformation.

Healthcare AI

Healthcare AI's Next Act: Prove It Or Move Aside

The healthcare sector is entering a trust inflection point. Clinical validation and clinician adoption, not algorithmic performance, will determine which AI investments survive the next 18 months.

The Reframing Question

If the governance gap is the real barrier, and 100-year-old hierarchy is the structure AI transformation must work around, what would your organisation look like if you designed the management layer first and the technology second?
